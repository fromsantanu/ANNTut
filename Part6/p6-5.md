# 📘 **Chapter 6.5: Transformers**

This chapter introduces one of the most important breakthroughs in modern AI:

👉 **Transformers — the architecture behind models like GPT, BERT, and modern AI systems**

---

## 🔹 1. Why Transformers?

Earlier models (RNN, LSTM):

* Process data sequentially
* Slow for long sequences
* Difficulty capturing long-range dependencies

---

👉 Transformers solve these problems using:

* **Attention mechanism**
* **Parallel processing**

---

### Simple Idea

👉 Instead of reading word by word:

* Look at **all words at once**
* Focus on important relationships

---

## 🔹 2. What is a Transformer?

### Definition

👉 A **Transformer** is a neural network architecture that:

* Uses attention instead of recurrence
* Processes entire sequences in parallel

---

### Analogy

Think of reading a paragraph:

* Instead of reading line by line
* You quickly scan and understand connections

👉 That’s how transformers work

---

## 🔹 4. Key Components of Transformer

---

## 🔸 4.1 Self-Attention

👉 Each word looks at all other words

---

### Example

Sentence:

> “The animal didn’t cross the street because it was tired”

👉 “it” refers to “animal”
👉 Attention helps identify this

---

## 🔸 4.2 Multi-Head Attention

👉 Multiple attention mechanisms run in parallel

---

### Why?

* Capture different relationships
* Learn richer representations

---

## 🔸 4.3 Positional Encoding

---

### Problem

Transformers don’t understand order by default

---

### Solution

👉 Add position information

---

### Idea

* Each word gets a positional value
* Helps model understand sequence

---

## 🔸 4.4 Feedforward Network

* Applied after attention
* Adds non-linearity

---

## 🔹 5. Transformer Block

Each block contains:

1. Multi-head attention
2. Add & normalize
3. Feedforward layer
4. Add & normalize

---

👉 Stacked multiple times → deep model

---

## 🔹 6. Attention Formula (Recap)

\text{Attention}(Q,K,V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V

---

👉 Core computation in transformers

## 🔹 10. Transformers vs RNN

| Feature      | RNN        | Transformer |

| Processing   | Sequential | Parallel    |
| Memory       | Limited    | Flexible    |
| Speed        | Slow       | Fast        |
| Long context | Weak       | Strong      |
