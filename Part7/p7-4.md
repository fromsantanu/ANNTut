# 📘 **Chapter 7.4: Model Evaluation Metrics**

This chapter answers a critical question:

👉 **How do we know if our model is actually good?**

---

## 🔹 1. Why Evaluation Metrics Matter

After training a model:

* We get predictions
* But we need to measure **how accurate and reliable they are**

---

### Simple Analogy

Think of an exam:

* Marks show performance
* Not just whether you attempted questions

👉 Metrics = **marks of your model**

---

## 🔹 2. Types of Problems

Evaluation depends on problem type:

---

### 🔸 Classification

* Output is category (Yes/No, Cat/Dog)

---

### 🔸 Regression

* Output is continuous (price, temperature)

---

👉 Different problems → different metrics

---

### Components

| Term                | Meaning                      |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| True Positive (TP)  | Correctly predicted positive |
| True Negative (TN)  | Correctly predicted negative |
| False Positive (FP) | Wrongly predicted positive   |
| False Negative (FN) | Missed positive              |

---

👉 Everything comes from this matrix

---

## 🔹 4. Accuracy

### Formula

[
$\text{Accuracy} = \frac{TP + TN}{TP + TN + FP + FN}$
]

---

### Meaning

👉 Percentage of correct predictions

---

### Problem

* Misleading for imbalanced data

---

### Example

* 95% non-disease → model always predicts “no disease”
* Accuracy = 95% (but useless!)

---

## 🔹 5. Precision

### Formula

[
$\text{Precision} = \frac{TP}{TP + FP}$
]

---

### Meaning

👉 Of predicted positives, how many are correct

---

### Use Case

* When false positives are costly

👉 Example: spam detection

---

## 🔹 6. Recall (Sensitivity)

### Formula

[
$\text{Recall} = \frac{TP}{TP + FN}$
]

---

### Meaning

👉 Of actual positives, how many were detected

---

### Use Case

* When missing a case is dangerous

👉 Example: disease detection

---

## 🔹 7. F1 Score

### Formula

[
$F1 = 2 \cdot \frac{Precision \cdot Recall}{Precision + Recall}$
]

---

### Meaning

👉 Balance between precision and recall

---

### Use Case

* When both are important

---

## 🔹 8. Precision vs Recall Tradeoff

---

### Example

* High precision → fewer false alarms
* High recall → fewer missed cases

---

👉 Need to balance based on problem

---

## 🔹 9. ROC Curve & AUC

---

### Idea

* Measures model performance at different thresholds

---

### AUC (Area Under Curve)

* 1 → perfect model
* 0.5 → random guessing

---

👉 Higher AUC = better model

---

# 🔹 PART B: Regression Metrics

---

## 🔹 10. Mean Squared Error (MSE)

MSE = \frac{1}{n} \sum (y - \hat{y})^2

---

👉 Penalizes large errors

---

## 🔹 11. Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE)

RMSE = \sqrt{MSE}

---

👉 Same unit as output

---

## 🔹 12. Mean Absolute Error (MAE)

MAE = \frac{1}{n} \sum |y - \hat{y}|

---

👉 Treats all errors equally

---

## 🔹 13. R² Score (Coefficient of Determination)

---

### Meaning

👉 Measures how well model explains data

---

### Range

* 1 → perfect
* 0 → no improvement

---

## 🔹 14. Choosing the Right Metric

| Problem                 | Metric         |
| ----------------------- | -------------- |
| Balanced classification | Accuracy       |
| Imbalanced data         | F1, Recall     |
| Regression              | MSE, RMSE, MAE |

---

## 🔹 15. Key Insight

👉 No single metric is best

👉 Choose based on:

* Problem
* Risk
* Application

---

## 🔹 16. Common Mistakes

---

### ❌ Using accuracy for imbalanced data

✔️ Use precision/recall

---

### ❌ Ignoring business context

✔️ Metrics depend on application

---

### ❌ Using only one metric

✔️ Use multiple metrics

---

## 🔹 17. Real-Life Example

### Medical Diagnosis

* False negative → very dangerous
* So recall is more important

---

## 🔹 18. Final Workflow

1. Train model
2. Predict on test data
3. Compute metrics
4. Compare models
5. Select best

---

## 🎯 Summary (In One Line)

👉 Model evaluation metrics measure how well a model performs and help choose the best model for real-world use.
