# 📘 **Chapter 5.2: Regularization Techniques**

In the previous chapter, we saw:

👉 Models can **overfit** (memorize data instead of learning patterns)

---

👉 **Regularization** is the solution.

It helps the model:

* Learn general patterns
* Avoid memorizing noise

---

## 🔹 1. What is Regularization?

### Definition

👉 Regularization is a set of techniques used to:

* Reduce overfitting
* Improve generalization

---

### Simple Analogy

Think of studying:

* Memorizing everything → overfitting
* Understanding concepts → generalization

👉 Regularization encourages **understanding, not memorizing**

---

## 🔹 2. Why Regularization Works

👉 Overfitting happens when:

* Model becomes too complex
* Weights become too large

---

👉 Regularization:

* Controls model complexity
* Keeps weights small

---

## 🔹 3. L2 Regularization (Ridge)

### Idea

👉 Penalize large weights

---

### Formula

L = L_0 + \lambda \sum w^2

---

### Meaning

* (L_0) → original loss
* (\lambda) → regularization strength
* (w) → weights

---

### Effect

* Large weights → high penalty
* Encourages smaller weights

---

### Intuition

👉 Like controlling volume:

* Prevent any one feature from dominating

---

## 🔹 4. L1 Regularization (Lasso)

### Idea

👉 Penalize absolute weights

---

### Formula

L = L_0 + \lambda \sum |w|

---

### Effect

* Some weights become exactly zero

---

### Advantage

👉 Performs **feature selection**

---

### Intuition

👉 Removes unnecessary inputs

---

## 🔹 5. L1 vs L2 Regularization

| Feature | L1                | L2              |
| ------- | ----------------- | --------------- |
| Penalty | Absolute          | Squared         |
| Effect  | Sparse model      | Smooth weights  |
| Use     | Feature selection | General purpose |

---

## 🔹 6. Dropout (Very Important)

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/g1XxPMSPdLiH9z8ldWt17In6LJrfNJUR5mKOZm80-gZ-vjAk_9RoD7KzUgYwA9A8h8k0E8aWOjFV6FkW7vuiWlxOXv1H3DMbir4C05JVNQEU9ObIW3YgS4pFOLGKdofw1IFjv2rQDp7keyQif2R0Avnmx0FWiZa0BpjJIYSy6MeXxsBAAU1DthaUU-HkR6JM?purpose=fullsize)

---

### Idea

👉 Randomly “turn off” some neurons during training

---

### How It Works

* At each step:

  * Some neurons are ignored
* Network cannot depend on specific neurons

---

### Benefit

* Reduces overfitting
* Improves robustness

---

### Analogy

👉 Like group study:

* Everyone must learn
* Cannot depend on one person

---

## 🔹 7. Early Stopping

### Idea

👉 Stop training before overfitting starts

---

### How?

* Monitor validation loss
* Stop when it increases

---

### Analogy

👉 Stop studying when you start getting tired

---

## 🔹 8. Data Augmentation

### Idea

👉 Create more training data

---

### Example (Images)

* Rotate
* Flip
* Crop

---

### Benefit

* More data → better generalization

---

## 🔹 9. Batch Normalization (Brief)

### Idea

👉 Normalize inputs in each layer

---

### Benefit

* Faster training
* Slight regularization effect

---

## 🔹 10. Choosing Regularization Techniques

### Common Practice

* Use **Dropout + L2**
* Add **Early stopping**

---

### When to Use L1

* When feature selection is needed

---

## 🔹 11. Key Insight

👉 Regularization does NOT improve training accuracy

👉 It improves:

* Test accuracy
* Real-world performance

---

## 🔹 12. Common Mistakes

### ❌ Using too much regularization

✔️ Can cause underfitting

---

### ❌ Ignoring λ (lambda)

✔️ Must be tuned carefully

---

### ❌ Using dropout in testing

✔️ Only used during training

---

## 🔹 13. Real-Life Example

### Predicting Disease

* Without regularization → memorizes patient data
* With regularization → learns real patterns

---

## 🎯 Summary (In One Line)

👉 Regularization techniques prevent overfitting by controlling model complexity and improving generalization.
