# 📘 **Chapter 5.5: Batch Normalization**

This chapter explains a powerful technique that makes training:

👉 **Faster, more stable, and more reliable**

---

## 🔹 1. What is Batch Normalization?

### Definition

👉 **Batch Normalization (BatchNorm)** is a technique that:

* Normalizes (scales) inputs to each layer
* Keeps values in a stable range during training

---

### Simple Idea

👉 Instead of letting values grow randomly:

* We **control and standardize them**

---

### Analogy

Think of cooking:

* If ingredients vary too much → taste becomes inconsistent
* Standardizing ingredients → consistent results

👉 BatchNorm ensures **consistent inputs**

---

## 🔹 2. Why Do We Need Batch Normalization?

During training:

* Each layer’s output keeps changing
* This is called **internal covariate shift**

---

👉 Problem:

* Slower learning
* Unstable gradients

---

👉 Solution:

* Normalize values at each layer

---

## 🔹 3. How Batch Normalization Works

---

### Step 1: Compute Mean

\mu = \frac{1}{m} \sum_{i=1}^{m} x_i

---

### Step 2: Compute Variance

\sigma^2 = \frac{1}{m} \sum_{i=1}^{m} (x_i - \mu)^2

---

### Step 3: Normalize

\hat{x} = \frac{x - \mu}{\sqrt{\sigma^2 + \epsilon}}

---

### Step 4: Scale and Shift

y = \gamma \hat{x} + \beta

---

### Meaning

* (\gamma) → scale
* (\beta) → shift

👉 These are **learnable parameters**

---

## 🔹 4. Key Idea

👉 Normalize → then allow model to adjust

---

👉 This ensures:

* Stability + flexibility

---

## 🔹 5. Where is BatchNorm Used?

👉 Typically placed:

* After linear/convolution layer
* Before activation

---

### Flow

```
Linear → BatchNorm → Activation
```

## 🔹 7. Benefits of Batch Normalization

---

### ✔️ 1. Faster Training

* Converges quickly

---

### ✔️ 2. Stable Learning

* Reduces gradient issues

---

### ✔️ 3. Allows Higher Learning Rate

* Less risk of divergence

---

### ✔️ 4. Regularization Effect

* Slightly reduces overfitting

---

## 🔹 8. BatchNorm vs No BatchNorm

| Without BatchNorm           | With BatchNorm   |
| --------------------------- | ---------------- |
| Slow training               | Faster training  |
| Unstable gradients          | Stable gradients |
| Sensitive to initialization | More robust      |

---

## 🔹 9. Training vs Testing Behavior

---

### During Training

* Uses batch statistics (mean & variance)

---

### During Testing

* Uses running averages

---

👉 Important difference

---

## 🔹 10. Common Mistakes

---

### ❌ Using BatchNorm after activation

✔️ Use before activation

---

### ❌ Ignoring batch size

✔️ Very small batches → unstable statistics

---

### ❌ Thinking BatchNorm replaces all regularization

✔️ Still use dropout if needed

---
