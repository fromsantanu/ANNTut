# 📘 **Chapter 8.1: Computer Vision**

This chapter introduces one of the most exciting applications of neural networks:

👉 **Computer Vision — enabling machines to “see” and understand images**

---

## 🔹 1. What is Computer Vision?

### Definition

👉 **Computer Vision (CV)** is a field of AI that allows computers to:

* Interpret images
* Understand visual content
* Make decisions based on visual data

---

### Simple Idea

👉 Just like humans use eyes to see,
computers use algorithms to “see” images

---

### Analogy

Think of a human:

* Sees a photo
* Recognizes objects

👉 Computer vision tries to do the same

---

## 🔹 2. Why is Computer Vision Important?

Used in many real-world applications:

* Self-driving cars
* Medical imaging
* Face recognition
* Security systems

---

👉 Vision is one of the most important senses → same for AI

---

## 🔹 3. How Computers See Images

---

### Step 1: Image as Numbers

👉 An image is just a matrix of pixel values

---

### Example

* Grayscale image → values from 0 to 255
* Color image → 3 channels (RGB)

---

### Representation

```id="j7k4xq"
Image → Matrix → Numbers
```

---

👉 Neural networks process numbers, not images

---

## 🔹 5. Role of Neural Networks

👉 CNNs are the backbone of computer vision

---

### Why CNN?

* Detect patterns (edges, shapes)
* Preserve spatial information

---

### Flow

1. Input image
2. Convolution layers
3. Feature extraction
4. Prediction

---

## 🔹 6. Typical Computer Vision Pipeline

---

### Step-by-Step

1. Load image
2. Preprocess (resize, normalize)
3. Pass through model (CNN)
4. Get prediction
5. Post-process results

---

### ❌ Occlusion

* Objects appear at different sizes

---

### ❌ Background noise

* Complex environments
