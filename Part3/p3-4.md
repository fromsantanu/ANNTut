# 📘 **Chapter 3.4: Optimization Algorithms**

In the previous chapter, we learned **Gradient Descent**.

👉 But basic gradient descent has problems:

* Slow learning
* Getting stuck
* Oscillations

---

👉 **Optimization algorithms are improved versions of gradient descent**
They help the model learn **faster, smoother, and more reliably**.

---

## 🔹 1. Why Do We Need Optimization Algorithms?

### Problems with Basic Gradient Descent

* Same learning rate for all directions
* Can get stuck in local minima
* Slow convergence

---

### Simple Analogy

Think of reaching the bottom of a valley:

* Basic GD → walking blindly
* Optimization algorithms → walking with **memory + smart steps**

---

## 🔹 2. Momentum

### Idea

👉 Use **past gradients (memory)** to speed up learning

---

### Intuition

* Instead of stopping at every step
* Build “momentum” like a rolling ball

---

### Formula

v = $\beta v + (1-\beta) \frac{\partial L}{\partial w}$

w = w - \eta v

---

### Benefit

* Faster convergence
* Reduces oscillation

---

### Analogy

👉 Like pushing a ball downhill—it keeps moving faster

---

## 🔹 3. AdaGrad (Adaptive Gradient)

### Idea

👉 Different learning rates for different parameters

---

### Intuition

* Frequently updated weights → smaller steps
* Rarely updated weights → larger steps

---

### Benefit

* Good for sparse data
* Automatic learning rate adjustment

---

### Problem

* Learning rate keeps shrinking → stops learning

---

## 🔹 4. RMSProp

### Idea

👉 Fix AdaGrad’s shrinking problem

---

### How?

* Uses moving average of squared gradients

---

### Formula

s = \beta s + (1-\beta) \left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial w}\right)^2

w = w - \frac{\eta}{\sqrt{s + \epsilon}} \frac{\partial L}{\partial w}

---

### Benefit

* Stable learning
* Works well in deep networks

---

## 🔹 5. Adam (Adaptive Moment Estimation)

👉 **Most popular optimizer today**

---

### Idea

Combines:

* Momentum (speed)
* RMSProp (adaptive learning)

---

### Formula (Simplified)

m = \beta_1 m + (1-\beta_1) g

v = \beta_2 v + (1-\beta_2) g^2

w = w - \eta \frac{m}{\sqrt{v} + \epsilon}

---

### Intuition

* Momentum → remembers direction
* RMSProp → adjusts step size

👉 Together → smart and efficient updates

## 🔹 7. Comparison of Optimizers

| Optimizer        | Speed     | Stability     | Use Case            |
| ---------------- | --------- | ------------- | ------------------- |
| Gradient Descent | Slow      | Stable        | Simple problems     |
| Momentum         | Faster    | Good          | Deep networks       |
| AdaGrad          | Moderate  | Decreasing LR | Sparse data         |
| RMSProp          | Fast      | Stable        | RNNs, deep learning |
| Adam             | Very Fast | Very Stable   | Most applications   |

---

## 🔹 8. Which Optimizer Should You Use?

### Default Choice

👉 **Adam**

---

### When to Try Others

* SGD + Momentum → better generalization
* RMSProp → sequence models

---

## 🔹 9. Key Insight

👉 Optimization algorithms improve:

* Speed of learning
* Stability of training
* Ability to reach better solutions

---

## 🔹 10. Common Mistakes

### ❌ Using default without understanding

✔️ Know why you use Adam

---

### ❌ Ignoring learning rate

✔️ Still important even with advanced optimizers

---

### ❌ Expecting optimizer to fix bad data

✔️ Data quality still matters most

---
